### Reply 1 - personal experience

Been down this exact road with my 14 Pro. Apple's number is rounded, so 80% on your screen can actually be 79.4 or 80.4 under the hood. The Genius Bar reads their own diagnostic, not your Settings page, and they'll absolutely turn you away at a displayed 80. What saved me a wasted trip was checking the real figure in iMazing first. It pulls raw design-vs-current capacity plus cycle count, so I walked in already knowing I was at 78.6% and they swapped it with no debate.

---

### Reply 2 - the practical distinction

Two things keep getting mixed up in here. Warranty replacement needs you under 80%. Paid replacement you can get any time, ever since the lawsuit. So if you just want it fresh and don't mind paying, book it and hand over the cash. If you're chasing the free AC+ swap, the diagnostic has to agree with you, and Settings rounds, which means it lies to you a little. I keep iMazing on my Mac and glance at the exact percentage before every appointment. Way cheaper than driving across town to get told "reads 80, come back later."
